Descartes seeding proposal

Seed the next generation of market‑neutral crypto strategies and unlock access to pure alpha.

1. Executive summary

DFi Labs seeks USD 20 million of seed capital to accelerate deployment of its Descartes strategy through a Cayman segregated portfolio on the Atitlan SPC platform. Descartes is a fully algorithmic, cross‑sectional momentum strategy: each day it takes long positions in the strongest‑performing liquid crypto‑assets and short positions in the weakest. By matching long and short exposures it delivers delta‑neutral returns uncorrelated with broad market movements. The strategy targets around USD 150 million of deployable capacity and offers investors pure alpha. Atitlan SPC is registered with the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA), providing robust governance and regulatory oversight.

2. Market opportunity & rationale

The digital‑asset market remains highly volatile and weakly correlated with other asset classes. This volatility, combined with the wide dispersion in returns across tokens, creates persistent relative‑value opportunities. Institutional infrastructure – prime brokerage, institutional custody, transparent price discovery and clearing – has matured considerably, enabling professional investors to access crypto markets with traditional risk controls. Cross‑sectional momentum strategies have delivered compelling risk‑adjusted returns across equities and now, increasingly, in digital assets. By comparing the relative strength of liquid tokens and arbitraging the spread between outperformers and underperformers, investors can capture inefficiencies while maintaining market neutrality.

  • Structural inefficiencies: crypto markets operate 24/7 with fragmented liquidity and disparate venues, leading to mis‑pricing and persistent relative‑performance anomalies.
  • Volatility & dispersion: high volatility and low cross‑correlation between tokens create an attractive backdrop for long/short strategies that capitalise on winners and losers.
  • Maturing market structure: institutional‑grade counterparties for custody, prime brokerage and fund administration reduce operational and counterparty risk.
  • Demand for diversified alpha: allocators seek uncorrelated return streams in crypto to complement directional exposure and hedge against market drawdowns.

3. Strategy description

Descartes is a systematic, market‑neutral strategy that harvests cross‑sectional momentum in liquid crypto‑assets. It is designed and implemented by DFi Labs’ quantitative research team and runs under a rigorous risk and governance framework.

3.1 Signal generation

  • Relative momentum: proprietary algorithms rank tokens by their recent risk‑adjusted performance, liquidity and micro‑structural factors. The top decile becomes the long portfolio and the bottom decile the short portfolio. Signals are refreshed intraday.
  • Machine learning: supervised models capture non‑linear relationships and regime shifts, enhancing signal robustness. Models are retrained regularly on de‑trended, de‑biased data to prevent overfitting.
  • Risk filters: coins with insufficient liquidity or exchange stability are excluded and position limits prevent excessive concentration.

3.2 Portfolio construction

  • Long/short basket: up to fifteen long and fifteen short positions are opened each day. Allocations are scaled to volatility and liquidity so that each position contributes proportionally to risk.
  • Delta‑neutrality: long and short gross exposures are matched to neutralise market beta. Positions are closed before the end of each day to avoid overnight risk.
  • Leverage: moderate leverage (1–2×) may be used via futures or margin lending, with strict haircut and liquidity limits. The strategy targets a Sharpe ratio above 5 and low drawdowns.

3.3 Execution & risk management

  • Data pipeline: high‑frequency trade, order‑book, derivatives pricing and on‑chain metrics feed into the signal engine via resilient infrastructure.
  • Smart routing: orders are executed through low‑latency connections to Tier‑1 exchanges and OTC desks, minimising slippage and fees.
  • Real‑time risk control: dashboards monitor gross/net exposures, venue limits, liquidity ladders and Value‑at‑Risk. Stress tests simulate 50 % price shocks, exchange failures and forced liquidation scenarios.
  • Pre‑trade checks: every order passes through a rules engine verifying compliance with risk limits, counterparty thresholds and regulatory restrictions.

4. Investment process

The investment process follows a disciplined chain from data to execution. Each step is automated yet overseen by professionals.

  1. Data collection & pre‑processing: continuous acquisition of trade, order‑book and on‑chain data for the universe of liquid crypto‑assets. Data is cleaned, normalised and stored in a high‑availability warehouse.
  2. Alpha modelling: statistical factors and machine‑ learning algorithms generate rankings based on relative performance, volatility, liquidity and network‑driven metrics.
  3. Portfolio optimisation: a linear optimisation engine selects 10–15 long and short positions with weights based on expected return, risk contribution and liquidity while keeping sector and beta exposures neutral.
  4. Execution: algorithmic trading engines route orders to exchanges and OTC desks via smart order‑routing and monitor execution quality through transaction cost analysis.
  5. Monitoring & rebalancing: positions are dynamically adjusted intra‑day in response to signal updates or liquidity changes. Full liquidation occurs before the daily close to neutralise overnight exposures.
  6. Risk oversight: the risk management team conducts daily Value‑at‑Risk, scenario and drawdown analyses and monitors exposure to each exchange or counterparty. Independent directors oversee adherence to the risk policy.

5. Fund terms & key features

The Descartes strategy is offered through a segregated portfolio of Atitlan SPC, ring‑fencing assets and liabilities. Key terms are summarised below.

  • Vehicle: segregated portfolio (SP) of Atitlan SPC, registered with CIMA. Assets and liabilities are ring‑fenced.
  • Seed capital: USD 20 million; target capacity above USD 150 million.
  • Investment minima & fees: minimum subscription USD 100 000 (negotiable); 2 % management fee on NAV; 20 % performance fee on net new profits with a high‑water mark, crystallised quarterly once seed capital is returned.
  • Subscriptions & redemptions: monthly subscriptions and redemptions with 30‑day notice. Seed investor locked for two years; other investors may have softer lock‑ups. In extreme market stress, gates or side pockets may be imposed.
  • Reporting: monthly NAV statements and flash performance (T+3), detailed risk and attribution reports, quarterly board letters and annual audited financial statements.

6. Structure & regulatory framework

The fund operates under the Atitlan SPC platform, a Cayman‑domiciled segregated portfolio company. Each SP within the SPC is ring‑fenced: liabilities are limited to the assets of that SP and other portfolios remain unaffected. The SPC is administered by Bolder Corporate Services and advised by BGA Law. It is registered with the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority and complies with the Mutual Funds Act, Anti‑Money Laundering Regulations and the CIMA Statement of Guidance on corporate governance.

Independent directors (for example, from Paradigm Governance Partners) ensure that fund operations and risk management meet best‑practice standards. The fund will appoint a Money‑Laundering Reporting Officer and AML Compliance Officer, maintain up‑to‑date policies on valuation, best execution, cyber‑security and conflicts of interest, and submit annual audited accounts.

8. Operational framework & governance

  • Administrator & NAV calculation: NAV Fund Services calculates NAV, maintains shareholder registers and provides a daily shadow NAV. It reconciles with custodians to ensure accuracy.
  • Audit: an annual audit of the SP by Grant Thornton (or another top‑tier firm) independently verifies assets, liabilities and valuations.
  • Valuation policy: primary prices are sourced from regulated exchanges and validated with secondary feeds. Pricing exceptions are reviewed by a valuation committee. Forks, airdrops and illiquid assets are treated according to IFRS/US GAAP.
  • Risk management: a dedicated risk committee chaired by Julien Boudier meets weekly to review VaR, stress tests, liquidity ladders and counterparty exposures and makes recommendations to the CIO.
  • Compliance & AML: the fund operates a full AML programme with KYC procedures and sanctions screening. A Money‑Laundering Reporting Officer and deputy monitor transactions and file suspicious activity reports as required.
  • Cyber‑security: multi‑layer security measures include cold and warm wallets, multi‑signature and MPC key management, DDoS protection and regular penetration testing. Insurance policies cover theft and cyber incidents.
  • Business continuity: disaster recovery plans, data‑centre redundancy and incident management procedures ensure continuity of operations.

  • Governance rights: option to appoint a board observer or independent director; veto rights over changes to strategy, leverage, service providers or key personnel; access to board minutes and policy updates.
  • Reporting: enhanced transparency including daily risk dashboards, weekly portfolio breakdowns, monthly detailed attribution and liquidity reports and quarterly board letters.

  6. Capital deployment: seed investor funds the SP; the strategy goes live; initial exposures are kept conservative during ramp‑up; weekly calls occur during the first two months.
  7. Ongoing governance: quarterly board meetings, annual audit and regular operational due‑diligence visits plus updates to policies and procedures.
